The Ayocote Negro Bean is thick skinned and very large. It's a firm bean but goes from dense to creamy with continued cooking.

The Ayocote Morado Bean thick-skinned runner bean is pretty and large. It's starchy but goes from dense to creamy with continued cooking.

Versatile and velvety, The Cranberry Bean produces a rich, indulgent bean broth, making it perfect for classic Italian dishes.

The Domingo Rojo Bean is a classic red bean, essential to dishes like Red Beans & Rice, and equally important to many Caribbean cuisines.

Thin-skinned and dense, the Hidasta Red Bean is an unusual red bean with a soft, creamy interior. Fantastic with brown rice and onions.

From California, the King City Pink Bean has a rich, dreamy bean broth. It has a thin skin and a dense, yet creamy interior.

The Large White Lima Bean is a large, new-crop, fast cooking Lima beans with classic Lima bean taste and texture.

The Mayocoba Bean has a pale yellow hue and super soft texture. This mild-flavored bean soaks up all the flavors of the cooking pot.

The Santa Maria Pinquito Bean produces a beefy bean broth and perfect for a barbecue, yet they're perfectly at home in meatless meals.

Preparation: Make sure you pick through your beans for any broken beans, pebbles, and other foreign substances. Rinse the good beans thoroughly.
Next, put the beans in a large pot. Add enough water or vegetable broth to cover the beans by at least two inches.
Let them soak for at least eight hours (over night is best). Then re-rinse the beans.
Return the beans to the pot, and cover them again with the same amount of water or veggie broth.
Turn on the high heat and wait until the mixture is boiling. Reduce the heat to medium low and cook for three hours, stirring occasionally. Remove the froth that accumulates on the surface or you will be Flatulence Freddie or Flatulence Francine for a few days.
Add seasoning, veggies, rice, and meat. Cook for another 30 minutes. Then, ENJOY!
